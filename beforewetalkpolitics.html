Before We Talk Politics:
What Actually Matters to You?

No parties. No candidates. No noise. Just you.

We all care about this country. We all want things to be better. But somewhere between what we actually believe and how we vote, something gets lost — in the noise, in the tribalism, in what we inherited from our families and our feeds.

This isn't a political quiz. It's a values exercise. You'll answer questions about what matters to you — before any political framing enters the picture. No party names. No politicians. Just statements about how the world works and how it should.

At the end, we'll show you which American political party your answers actually align with. You might be surprised. Most people are.

We'll also show you how your values compare to the core teachings of Jesus of Nazareth — not as a religious judgment, but because his moral framework is one of the most thoroughly documented ethical systems in Western history, and the gap between what he taught and what gets done in his name is worth examining.
